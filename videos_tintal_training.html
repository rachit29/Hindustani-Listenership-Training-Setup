Tintal Rhythm Training Videos for Group 1

Tintal (or teental, trital; Hindi: तीन ताल) is the most common tala of Hindustani music. Tintal has sixteen (16) beats in four equal divisions(4+4+4+4). The first beat out of 16 beats is called the "Sam"

>>>>> To count the Tintal,try to mimic the hand gestures in the following videos.

>>>Clap on the first beat, clap on the 5th beat, then wave on the 9th beat and lastly again clap on the 13th beat; these three claps (in Hindi "tin" meaning 'three' + tāl "clap") give the rhythm its name.

IMPORTANT: Keep in mind that the goal is to find the "Sam" which is represented by "Dha" on the top of rhythm circle. Try to learn the timing of the repeating "Sam" with the aid of "Hand Gestures"
